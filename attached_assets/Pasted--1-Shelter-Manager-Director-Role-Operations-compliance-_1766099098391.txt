🐾 1. Shelter Manager / Director

Role: Operations, compliance, outcomes, people

7:30–8:30 AM — Open & Triage

Checks overnight notes (medical issues, emergencies)

Reviews:

Dogs stuck too long in intake or medical

Staffing gaps for the day

Critical supplies (food, meds, kennel space)

Makes quick decisions:

Intake pause?

Emergency foster call?

Vet escalation?

Stress point: Everything feels urgent. No clear “what matters most.”

9:00–11:00 AM — Decision Work

Approves:

Adoptions

Euthanasia decisions (hardest part)

Transfers to partner rescues

Handles angry emails or public complaints

Meets with medical lead or behavior team

What software should do

Surface only true blockers

Show length-of-stay risks

Make consequences visible (e.g., “3 dogs at risk this week”)

1:00–3:00 PM — Admin & Strategy

Budget, grants, donors

City/county reporting

Board communication

Crisis prevention

Biggest pain: Drowning in reports that don’t drive action.

4:00–6:00 PM — Firefighting

Intake surprises

Staff burnout

Medical complications

Foster failures

Manager success = fewer surprises

🩺 2. Veterinary / Medical Staff

Role: Keep animals healthy enough to move forward

8:00–9:00 AM — Medical Rounds

Walk kennel rows

Identify:

Sick dogs

Weight loss

Behavioral stress from illness

Prioritize who must be seen today

Stress point: Too many animals, not enough time.

9:30–12:00 PM — Treatments

Vaccines

Medications

Wound care

Intake exams

Record keeping (often rushed)

Pain

Duplicate data entry

Forgetting follow-ups

No visibility into downstream impact

1:00–3:00 PM — Procedures

Spay/neuter prep

Surgery

Recovery checks

3:00–5:00 PM — Documentation & Planning

Log treatments

Schedule next doses

Flag animals not ready for adoption

What software should do

Auto-schedule next steps

Prevent dogs from advancing prematurely

Reduce documentation friction

🧠 3. Behavior Team / Trainers

Role: Decide if a dog is adoptable and how

Morning — Observation

Watch dogs in kennels

Note:

Reactivity

Fear

Aggression

Improvement or regression

Stress

One bad call can mean a returned adoption or bite incident

Midday — Evaluations

Formal behavior assessments

Dog-to-dog tests

Human interaction tests

Afternoon — Plans & Notes

Create behavior plans

Recommend foster vs adoption vs rescue transfer

Communicate risks to adoption team

What software should do

Track behavior over time (not just one score)

Show context (medical, length of stay)

Prevent “checkbox behavior scoring”

🐕 4. Animal Care Attendants / Kennel Staff

Role: Daily care and cleaning (the backbone of the shelter)

7:00–11:00 AM — Cleaning & Feeding

Clean kennels

Feed animals

Walk dogs

Spot health issues early

Stress

Physically exhausting

Emotionally heavy

Often underpaid

Midday — Enrichment & Support

Socialization

Playgroups

Basic training

Assist medical/behavior teams

Late Afternoon — Reset

Re-clean

Prep for next day

Update notes (often minimal time)

What software should do

Simple checklists

Minimal typing

Voice notes or quick taps

🏡 5. Foster Coordinator

Role: Keep animals out of kennels

Morning — Foster Check-ins

Follow up on:

Medical fosters

Behavior fosters

Emergency placements

Manage returns or issues

Midday — Matching

Match dogs to fosters

Train new fosters

Coordinate supplies

Afternoon — Crisis Management

“I can’t keep the dog”

“The dog is sick”

“The dog bit my cat”

Stress

High emotional labor

Constant urgency

What software should do

Show foster capacity clearly

Track foster risk

Reduce last-minute emergencies

❤️ 6. Adoption Counselors / Front Desk

Role: Match animals with people (and prevent returns)

Opening — Applications

Review new applications

Flag red/yellow/green

Schedule meet & greets

Midday — Meet & Greets

Introduce dogs to families

Answer hard questions

Manage expectations

Late Day — Follow-ups

Rejections

Approvals

Education

Documentation

Stress

Emotional customers

Fear of making the wrong match

What software should do

Show dog readiness clearly

Surface risks honestly

Reduce back-and-forth

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 7. Volunteers

Role: Multiply capacity

When They Arrive

Need clear instructions

Need simple tasks

Need to feel useful

What software should do

Show “what needs help right now”

Avoid overwhelming detail

Keep permissions tight
